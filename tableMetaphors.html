what is a rock? a natural thing that sprinkles the surface, sometimes large |a divider|, and sometimes small |stepped on|.
what is an island? a lonely thing surrounded by a large, vaster thing. sometimes it is clustered with other lonely things like itself.
what is a building? an often tall thing with levels that was built by moving things to contain moving things.
what is a river? a long contained, changing thing that winds around other things unlike it. moving things like this thing.
what is a person? a moving thing that likes to move other things very much.
what is an animal? a moving thing that consumes smaller things.
what is a plant? a slowly changing thing.
what is a hole? a thing that something is missing.
what is a country? a large, invisible thing with edges, delineated by moving things.
what is a street? moving things follow this long, splitting thing that makes them a little faster.
what is a feeling? a complex, invisible thing attached to moving things that affects their speed.
what is a sun? a thing that makes other things pretty and visible.
what is a day? an invisible thing made for counting the movements of moving things.
what is a year? a large invisible thing containing a series of invisible things made for counting the movements of moving things.
what is a question? an invisible thing moving things excrete when confused.
what is a job? an invisible thing that makes moving things move.
what is a life? a whole lot of things stacked atop one another.
what is a universe? a large variety of things packed tightly inside and around and next to each other.

note: metaphors work because they are made with context (like ?), they make sense in contained examples, but may break down when extended.
